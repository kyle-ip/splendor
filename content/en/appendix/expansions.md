Base Splendor is the main tutorial content. Below is a brief overview of **Cities of Splendor** and related modules. They are independent of base strategy and can be added module by module.

## Cities of Splendor

The box includes four optional modules, usable alone or combined with the base game:

### Cities

- Adds city tiles with alternate victory conditions or extra scoring paths
- **Vs base strategy**: Splits the finish line—no longer solely racing to 15 points

### Trading Posts

- Variants that change how you take gems
- **Vs base strategy**: Gem-economy chapters need adjusting to the new rules

### The Great Cities

- Special development cards with unique rewards
- **Vs base strategy**: Card evaluation weights must include the new rewards

### The Nobles

- Extra noble tiles for more path diversity
- **Vs base strategy**: Noble planning still applies; combinations are richer

## Splendor Duel (Two-Player Standalone)

- A separate 2-player game, not a base-game expansion
- Includes draft, a shared track, and different victory conditions
- Learn it on its own; do not mix with base Splendor

## Usage Tips

1. Master base Splendor for 10+ games first
2. Add **one** module at a time; stack only after you're comfortable
3. When returning to Parts 2–3 of this guide, ask which strategies still hold under the module

There are also three **unofficial solo modes** (fixed-fund challenge / dice automa / Solo Automa)—see the sidebar "Solo Modes." Original Solo Automa print-and-play files live in the repo at `extensions/solo/`.

> Expansion coverage will keep growing. Feel free to note module-specific lessons from your games.

## Board Game Arena

[Board Game Arena — Splendor](https://boardgamearena.com/gamepanel?game=splendor) supports several official variants. Brief BGA-aligned summaries:

| Variant | What it adds |
|---------|----------------|
| **Cities** | City tiles that replace or alter the usual 15-point finish |
| **Trading Posts** | Special bonuses that change gem-taking and related actions |
| **Orient** | Extra development cards and effects from the Orient expansion line |
| **Strongholds** | Placement / control elements that interact with the display and scoring |

BGA also notes: **tiebreak** = fewest development cards; you may **reserve with no gold** if gold is empty; **nobles cannot be refused** (choose if several); **end the round** so everyone had equal turns; **competitor mode** turns assists off.
