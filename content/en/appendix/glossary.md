Alphabetical index of terms used across this guide. **Official rule terms** match the Splendor / Splendor Duel rulebooks; **strategy terms** are how this tutorial explains ideas in Parts II–IV.

---

## Base Splendor — Components & Setup

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Bank** | The piles of gem and gold tokens in the center of the table. |
| **Bonus** | The gem color shown on the top-right of a development card. Each bonus of a color reduces future costs of that color by 1 and counts toward noble requirements. |
| **Development card** | A card you buy for prestige and bonuses. Three levels: Level 1 (40 cards), Level 2 (30), Level 3 (20). |
| **Display** | The face-up development cards in the middle (4 per level when decks allow). |
| **Gold (joker)** | Wild token; substitutes for any one gem color when paying. Obtained only by reserving (if any gold remains). |
| **Gem / token colors** | Emerald (green), sapphire (blue), ruby (red), diamond (white), onyx (black), gold (yellow). |
| **Noble tile** | A face-up tile that visits a player who has enough bonuses of the required colors. Worth **3 prestige**; cannot be refused; at most **1 visit per turn**. |
| **Prestige points** | Score on development cards and nobles. Reaching **≥15** triggers the endgame round. |
| **Reserve / reserved card** | A development card kept in your hand (max **3**). The only way to get gold. Reserved cards cannot be discarded—only bought. |

## Base Splendor — Rules & Flow

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Buy (purchase)** | Pay a card’s cost using bonuses + tokens; spent tokens return to the bank. |
| **Endgame round** | When someone reaches ≥15 prestige, finish the **current round** so every player has taken the same number of turns, then compare scores. |
| **Noble visit** | Automatic at the **end of your turn** if your bonuses meet a noble’s requirements. Not an action. If several qualify, choose one. |
| **Take 2 same color** | Allowed only if **≥4** tokens of that color remain in the bank when you take them. |
| **Take 3 different colors** | One token each of three different gem colors (not gold). |
| **10-token limit** | At the **end of your turn**, you may hold at most **10** tokens total (including gold). Return excess to the bank. |
| **Tiebreak** | If prestige is tied, the player who purchased the **fewest development cards** wins. |

## Strategy Terms (this guide)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Card evaluation / valuation** | Judging whether a card is worth buying—points, engine value, noble help, and timing—not only printed prestige. |
| **Denial** | Slowing an opponent by buying, reserving, or taking gems they need. Most effective in **2-player** games. |
| **Engine** | Your accumulated **bonuses** that let you buy cards at lower cost. Level 1 cards often build the engine; Level 2/3 are “output.” |
| **Endgame calc** | Planning the final turns: who triggers at 15, who still acts, and whether to buy for points or tiebreak card count. |
| **Hybrid path** | Opening that mixes noble progress with a focused bonus color instead of pure noble sprint or mono-color. |
| **Level economy** | How card **level** (1 / 2 / 3) affects cost, points, and when each level is worth buying. |
| **Mono-color engine** | Building many bonuses in one color to unlock cheap high-level buys or a noble. |
| **Noble planning / noble sprint** | Choosing and racing toward specific nobles based on bonus requirements. |
| **Opening** | Your first ~5 turns: gem takes, early buys, and which path (noble / mono / hybrid) you commit to. |
| **Reading the table** | Inferring opponents’ plans from visible bonuses, tokens, reserves (count), and the display. |
| **Reservation tactics** | Using reserve for gold, denial, or securing a future buy—not hoarding dead cards. |
| **Tempo** | How fast you convert resources into points and engine relative to opponents (accelerate vs. stockpile). |
| **Unique answer** | The only card on the display that completes your noble or key buy—high priority to buy or reserve. |
| **Worked spot / board example** | A short table in a lesson showing numbers and a recommended line. |

## Splendor Duel

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Copy-color card** | A Jewel card ability: stack on an existing bonus card; this card’s bonus counts as that color (for costs and monocolor prestige). |
| **Crown** | Printed on some Jewel cards. At your **3rd** and **6th** Crown total, take a **Royal card**. **≥10 Crowns** is a win condition. |
| **Gold (Duel)** | Wild for gems **and Pearls**. Only gained via “take 1 Gold + reserve 1 card” while Gold remains on the board. |
| **Jewel card** | Duel’s development cards (67 total, 3 levels), shown in a **pyramid** (3 / 4 / 5 face-up). |
| **Mandatory action** | Each turn you must do one: take adjacent tokens, Gold+reserve, or purchase. |
| **Monocolor prestige win** | **≥10 prestige** on cards of one bonus color (copy-color cards count with their stack). |
| **Optional action** | Before the mandatory action: use Privilege(s), and/or Replenish the board (order fixed). |
| **Pearl** | Token type with no matching bonus; some costs require Pearls (or Gold as wild). |
| **Privilege** | Scroll (max 3 per player). Spend to take a gem/Pearl from the board; also passed to opponent when you take 3 same-color tokens, 2 Pearls, or Replenish. |
| **Pyramid** | Face-up Jewel layout: 3 Level-3, 4 Level-2, 5 Level-1 below the Victory tile. |
| **Replenish** | Refill empty board spaces from the bag (center → spiral); opponent takes 1 Privilege. |
| **Royal card** | Claimed at 3rd / 6th Crown; grants abilities/prestige per card text; not an action. |
| **Spiral (token board)** | Tokens sit on a shared board; takes must be **adjacent** in a straight line (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal). |
| **Victory tile** | Shows Duel’s three win conditions: **20 prestige**, **10 Crowns**, or **10 same-color prestige**. Game ends **immediately** when you meet one at end of turn. |

Full rules: [Part V · Duel](/learn/duel).

## Expansions (Cities of Splendor)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Cities / city tile** | Replaces nobles; requires prestige **and** bonus combos; claiming one can end the game. |
| **Orient (cards)** | Extra development cards beside each level; wider market and special effects. |
| **Strongholds** | Pieces that contest cards on the display beyond normal buying. |
| **Trading Posts** | Board with crests that unlock special abilities after requirements are met. |

## Solo Modes (unofficial)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Automa** | A solo opponent (dice or card-driven) that buys or takes from the display. |
| **Dice automa (Mode 2)** | Roll die when automa cannot buy; may take a Level 1 slot or gold. |
| **Fixed-fund challenge (Mode 1)** | Start with 18 tokens; never take from the bank again; nobles are **reset charges**. |
| **Reset charge** | In Mode 1, spending a noble to discard the display and flip 4 new Level 1 cards. |
| **Solo Automa (Mode 3)** | Card-driven automa using AI action cards; full 2-player layout. |

Details: [Solo modes](/reference/solo).
