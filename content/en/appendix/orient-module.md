Optional deep-dive after you know base Splendor. **Orient** (from *Cities of Splendor*) adds Orient cards beside each level. Use **one** module at a time. Overview: [Expansions](/reference/expansions).

## What Changes

- Each level can show **4 base + 2 Orient** face-up (when decks allow)
- Orient effects (double bonus, free Level 1 take, etc.) change card evaluation
- Refills pull from Orient decks as well as base decks

## Evaluation Shift

| Base habit | With Orient |
|------------|-------------|
| Rank by points ÷ remaining cost | Rank by **effect + noble fit**, then points |
| Single bonus = +1 color | Double-bonus / free take can be the whole plan |
| Narrow display | Wider market—scan Orient slots every refill |

## Worked Spot — Double blue

Nobles need blue×3. Orient shows a 0-pt with **double blue**. Base row shows a 3-pt needing mixed costs.

| Choice | Prefer |
|--------|--------|
| 3-pt base | Only if you are already racing to 15 this turn |
| Double-blue Orient | Early/mid—two blue for noble + engine in one buy |

## Worked Spot — Free Level 1 take

An Orient card lets you take a free Level 1 after purchase. Display has a cheap green you need for a 3/3/3.

| Choice | Prefer |
|--------|--------|
| Buy Orient then free green | If the free take finishes a noble color |
| Ignore Orient for a naked Level 2 | Only when the Level 2 cashes points **this turn** near 15 |

## Counter-example — Flashy trap

You buy every Orient with text while ignoring base Level 2 points. Opponent converts a simple green–red engine into nobles and Level 2s. Effects that don’t feed nobles or the race are distractions.

## Try It

Play one base game with Orient only (no other Cities modules). Afterward, note in [replay notes](/tools/replay): which Orient effect actually changed a buy decision.
