Each noble tile is worth **3 prestige**. When you meet a noble’s requirements, that noble visits **automatically** at the end of your turn—it is not an action.

## Two Patterns

The base game has **10** nobles. Half use each pattern:

| Pattern | Count | Example |
|---------|------:|---------|
| **4 / 4** — four bonuses in each of **two** colors | 5 | Emerald ×4, ruby ×4 |
| **3 / 3 / 3** — three bonuses in each of **three** colors | 5 | Emerald ×3, sapphire ×3, onyx ×3 |

Both patterns score the same (+3). The 4/4 tiles push you toward a **taller** two-color engine; the 3/3/3 tiles reward a **wider** three-color spread.

## Trigger Conditions

When your development-card bonuses meet a tile’s amounts (**bonuses only**—tokens in hand do not count), that noble visits at the end of your turn.

## Important Rules

- Nobles **do not consume** bonuses or tokens; they only check your bonus totals
- You **cannot refuse** a visit
- At most **1 noble** per turn (if you qualify for several, choose one)
- Place the tile face up in front of you; it is not returned to the supply

## Strategy Tip (Beginner)

At setup, scan every face-up noble and note which colors appear often—especially colors on **both** a 4/4 and a 3/3/3. You don’t have to force nobles, but if your path naturally aligns, 3 points is very efficient.

## Common Misconception

> “You must hand over matching gems to attract a noble.” — **False**  
> Only bonuses matter; keep your tokens for buying cards.
