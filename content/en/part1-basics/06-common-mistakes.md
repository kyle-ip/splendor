Avoid these pitfalls and your first game will go much more smoothly.

## 1. Only Taking Gems, Never Buying

Gems are a means, not the goal. Every 2–3 turns, try to buy a card and build bonuses.

## 2. Ignoring Nobles

Nobles at +3 are highly efficient. Scan them at setup; if a path naturally fits, plan around it.

## 3. Chasing High-Point Cards Too Early

Level 2/3 high scorers are expensive. Forcing them without a bonus base slows you down.

## 4. Forgetting the 10-Token Limit

Before taking gems, check: will you go over 10? If so, buy first or skip taking gems.

## 5. Reserving Too Many Useless Cards

You only have 3 reserve slots. Every reserved card should have a clear buy plan.

## 6. Not Watching Display Refills

Bought cards are replaced immediately from the deck. Opponents' buys change your options—scan the display each turn.

## Chapter Takeaways

- Four actions: 3 different / 2 same / reserve / buy
- Discounts stack permanently; buying gets cheaper over time
- Nobles check bonus totals for +3
- 15 points triggers the endgame; highest score wins
