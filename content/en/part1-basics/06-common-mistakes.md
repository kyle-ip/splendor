Avoid these pitfalls and your first game will go much more smoothly.

## 1. Only Taking Tokens, Never Buying

Tokens are a means, not the goal. Every 2–3 turns, try to buy a card and build bonuses.

## 2. Ignoring Nobles

Nobles give +3 prestige and cost no action. Scan them at setup; if a path naturally fits, plan around it.

## 3. Chasing High-Point Cards Too Early

Level 2/3 high scorers are expensive. Forcing them without a bonus base slows you down.

## 4. Misreading the 10-Token Limit

At the end of your turn you may hold at most 10 tokens (including gold). Going over is allowed **during** the turn—you simply return the excess before you finish.

Before you take tokens, still ask: will returning leftovers leave me where I want? If not, buy or reserve instead of taking.

## 5. Filling Reserve Slots with Useless Cards

You only have 3 reserve slots. Every reserved card should have a clear buy plan. Reserved cards cannot be discarded—only bought.

## 6. Not Watching Display Refills

Bought or reserved cards are replaced immediately from that level’s deck. Scan the new card each turn—opportunities often appear there.

## Worked Mistakes

### Hand overflow after a take

You hold **9 tokens** and take **3 different**. Mid-turn you briefly have 12—that is legal. Before ending the turn you must return **2**. Returning the two colors you still need for next buy is the classic trap: take only when the leftover hand still supports your plan, or buy/reserve instead.

### Forgetting bonuses

A Level 2 costs **6 green**. You already have **+3 green** bonus and **2 green + 1 gold** in hand. Paying as if you need all 6 from the hand (and deciding you “cannot afford it”) is a common misread—net cost is **3 green**, so hand + gold covers it.

### Wrong noble race

Nobles in play include **3 green / 3 blue / 3 black**. You buy a fourth **red** bonus while sitting on 2/2/2 toward that noble. The red card may be fine for points later, but this turn you delayed the +3 visit that was one focused buy away—scan noble gaps before “whatever is cheapest.”

## Chapter Takeaways

- Four actions: 3 different / 2 same / reserve / buy
- Bonuses stack permanently; buying gets cheaper over time
- Nobles check bonus totals only; visit for +3
- 15 prestige ends the round; highest score wins (fewest cards breaks ties)
