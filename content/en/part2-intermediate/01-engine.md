Win rate in Splendor depends heavily on your **engine**—whether your bonus engine can support steady buying.

## What Is an Engine

An **engine** is the bonus combination you build so you can buy more cards at lower cost.

- **Level 1 cards**: Mostly 0–1 points; the "infrastructure" of your engine
- **Level 2/3 cards**: High points; the "output" side, which needs the engine to support them

## Engine-Building Principles

1. **Build first, then sprint**: In the first 5–8 turns, prefer 0-point bonus cards over naked 3-point scorers
2. **Focus colors**: Don't spread evenly across five colors—pick 2–3 as your main axis
3. **Cost hedging**: Prefer cards whose bonuses help pay for cards you want next

## Board Example

The display has a 0-point Level 1 (+1 emerald, cost 1 ruby 1 sapphire) and a 3-point Level 2 (cost 3 ruby 3 sapphire 2 onyx).

**Casual approach**: Buy the 0-point emerald; next turns cost less.  
**Competitive approach**: If a noble needs 3 emerald, that 0-point card is a key link on the noble path—higher priority than the 3-point card.

## Signs Your Engine Is Broken

- Three turns in a row you can only take gems, never buy
- Your hand is often stuck at 10 tokens
- Opponents have 6+ cards while you have only 2

If any of these appear, buy next turn—even a 0-point bonus card.
