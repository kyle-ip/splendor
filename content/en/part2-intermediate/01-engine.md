Win rate in Splendor depends heavily on your **engine**—whether your bonus engine can support steady buying.

## What Is an Engine

An **engine** is the bonus combination you build so you can buy more cards at lower cost.

- **Level 1 cards**: Mostly 0–1 points; the “infrastructure” of your engine
- **Level 2/3 cards**: High points; the “output” side, which needs the engine to support them

## Engine-Building Principles

1. **Build first, then sprint**: In the first 5–8 turns, prefer 0-point bonus cards over naked 3-point scorers
2. **Focus colors**: Don’t spread evenly across five colors—pick 2–3 as your main axis
3. **Cost hedging**: Prefer cards whose bonuses help pay for cards you want next

## Worked Board A — Noble-fit Level 1

| Card | Prestige | Bonus | Cost |
|------|----------|-------|------|
| A (Level 1) | 0 | +1 green | 1 red + 1 blue |
| B (Level 2) | 3 | +1 red | 3 red + 3 blue + 2 black |

Nobles in play need **3 green**. You have almost no bonuses yet.

| Approach | Choice | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| Casual | Buy A | Cheaper now; next buys cost less |
| Competitive | Buy A | Green is on the noble path—higher priority than the naked 3 points on B |

Buying B without an engine often leaves you taking tokens for several turns afterward.

## Worked Board B — Axis vs five-color spray

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| You (T5) | Bonuses: green 1, red 1, blue 1, white 1, black 0 |
| Display | Cheap +1 green; expensive mono white; Level 2 needing green×2 blue×2 |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Buy another off-axis color | Five thin piles—still can’t cash Level 2 |
| Stack green or blue | You finish a noble color **and** unlock the Level 2 |

**Rule:** Mid-early, deepen 2–3 colors that appear on nobles and on the Level 2s you want—don’t collect one of each.

## Worked Board C — Broken engine repair

Three turns in a row you can only take tokens. Hand sits at 8–9 gems; bonuses total 3.

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Take again toward a distant 5 | Hand hits 10; still no buy |
| Buy any affordable 0-pt bonus | Engine restarts; next turns can buy |

If you see the broken-engine signs below, **force a buy next turn**.

## Counter-example — Naked 3 too early

Turn 4 you buy a 3-pt Level 2 with almost no bonuses. Opponents keep buying Level 1s. By turn 10 they cash two Level 2s while you still take gems for every purchase. The early 3 looked ahead on the scoreboard and **lost the race**.

## Signs Your Engine Is Broken

- Three turns in a row you can only take tokens, never buy
- Your hand is often stuck near 10 tokens
- Opponents have 6+ cards while you have only 2

## Key Takeaways

- Early: bonuses over naked points
- Pick a 2–3 color axis tied to nobles
- Repair a stalled engine with any affordable buy

Practice color stacking in [fixed capital](/tools/solo/fixed) or [standard practice](/tools/standard).
