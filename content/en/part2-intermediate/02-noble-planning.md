Nobles are among Splendor's **most action-efficient** scoring plays (+3 prestige without spending a turn to buy points).

## Opening Scan Method

Before play starts, spend 30 seconds on all nobles:

1. Count **4/4** vs **3/3/3** tiles on the table—two-color depth vs three-color width
2. Which colors appear **≥2 times**? (e.g. three nobles all need sapphire)
3. Which path has matching bonus cards in the **Level 1 display**?

A color that sits on both a 4/4 and a 3/3/3 is especially “hot”: investing there can keep two noble paths alive.

## Working Backward

**3/3/3 example** (Francis I style): emerald 3, ruby 3, onyx 3 → plan three cheap bonuses in those colors.

**4/4 example** (Mary Stuart style): emerald 4, ruby 4 → plan a taller green–red engine; random third colors dilute the sprint.

Scan Level 1 for the **missing colors**, not just any cheap card.

## Worked Spot

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| Nobles on table | Francis I (emerald×3 ruby×3 onyx×3); Charles V (ruby×3 diamond×3 onyx×3); Mary Stuart (emerald×4 ruby×4) |
| Level 1 display | Two cheap emerald bonuses, one cheap ruby, no onyx |
| Your bonuses | None yet |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Chase Francis I hard | Emerald is open, but onyx is missing—sprint may stall |
| Soft toward Charles V | Ruby is available; still need onyx (and diamond) from refills |
| Lean Mary Stuart | Emerald + ruby already show; 4/4 needs more depth but skips onyx for now |
| Hybrid | Take one emerald + one ruby; wait one refill before locking a noble |

**Conclusion:** Prefer the noble whose **missing colors already appear** in Level 1. If a color is absent, keep the path soft until the display refills—or pivot to a 4/4 that ignores that color.

## Casual vs Competitive

| Perspective | Advice |
|-------------|--------|
| Casual | Take nobles "along the way"; don't force them |
| Competitive | If the opening display fits, run a **noble sprint** (first noble in 7–9 turns) |

## Multiplayer Contention

In four-player games, popular-color nobles may be chased by several players. If an opponent clearly leads on those bonuses, **switch paths**—don't stubbornly contest.
