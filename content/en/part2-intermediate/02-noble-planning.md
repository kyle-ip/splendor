Nobles are among Splendor's **most action-efficient** scoring plays (+3 prestige without spending a turn to buy points).

## Opening Scan Method

Before play starts, spend 30 seconds on all nobles:

1. Which colors appear **≥2 times**? (e.g. three nobles all need sapphire)
2. Is there a noble your **current bonuses** can connect to?
3. Which path has matching bonus cards in the **Level 1 display**?

## Working Backward

Target noble: emerald 3, sapphire 3, onyx 3

Backward: you need at least +3 emerald, +3 sapphire, and +3 onyx bonuses.  
Scan the Level 1 display and plan 2–3 key cheap cards—don't buy at random.

## Casual vs Competitive

| Perspective | Advice |
|-------------|--------|
| Casual | Take nobles "along the way"; don't force them |
| Competitive | If the opening display fits, run a **noble sprint** (first noble in 7–9 turns) |

## Multiplayer Contention

In four-player games, popular-color nobles may be chased by several players. If an opponent clearly leads on those bonuses, **switch paths**—don't stubbornly contest.

Use the **noble tracker** tool: enter your bonuses and see which nobles you can attract.
