Taking gems looks passive, but it is the core of tempo control.

## When to Take Gems

- **For next turn's buy**: You're 1–2 short; take them and buy next turn
- **To deny an opponent**: Take colors they urgently need (especially in 2-player)
- **To keep your hand flowing**: At 7+ gems with no buy, consider buying a cheap card before taking more

## 3 Different vs 2 Same

| Action | Best when |
|--------|-----------|
| 3 different | Costs are spread, you need several colors, bank is full |
| 2 same | You're clearly short one color, bank has ≥4, buying a big card next turn |

## Denial Awareness

**Casual players**: You can ignore this and focus on your own path.  
**Competitive players**: Denial weight is **high** in 2-player; in 3–4 player, only when "the opponent is 1 gem from a key card."

## Board Example

Opponent has 2 sapphire bonuses. The only +2 sapphire Level 1 in the display costs 2 sapphire. Bank has 3 sapphire left.

You can't take 2 sapphire (needs bank ≥4). Take 1 sapphire plus other colors, or **buy/reserve** that card yourself.

## Value of Gold

Gold = flexibility. Keep 1–2 gold for cost gaps, but don't hoard it unused for long.
