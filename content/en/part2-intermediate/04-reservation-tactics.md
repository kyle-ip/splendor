Reserving is not only “locking a card”—it’s a tempo and denial tool, and the only way to gain **gold**.

## Three Main Uses

1. **Snatch key cards**: Unique answers in the display, or cards an opponent needs
2. **Get gold**: When 1 short, reserve for +1 gold and buy next turn
3. **Hide intent**: Deck-top reserves keep opponents guessing about your path

## Worked Spot A — Gold bridge

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| Card in display | Level 2, 3 prestige, cost 2 blue + 2 red after your bonuses |
| Your tokens | 1 blue, 2 red, 0 gold |
| Opponent next | Can afford that card this round |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Take tokens | Opponent likely buys the card |
| Reserve now | You take 1 gold; next turn you can pay 1 blue + 2 red + 1 gold |

Reserve when the **next-turn buy is real**. If you still need three more turns of taking tokens, prefer building toward the cost instead of burning a slot.

## Worked Spot B — 2p denial reserve

Seat order: You → Opponent. A Level 2 on display finishes their noble (+3) and they can buy it next. You cannot afford it.

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Take gems for your own Level 2 | They cash noble; tempo collapses |
| Reserve their noble answer (+gold) | You deny +3 and may cash that card yourself later |

In 2p, denial ROI is high—one reserve can be worth more than a soft buy.

## Worked Spot C — 4p: don’t burn slots

Four players. You already hold two vague reserves. A cheap Level 1 you need appears.

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Reserve it (third slot) | Hand full; next unique answer has nowhere to go |
| Buy it now if affordable | Free a future denial slot |

At 4p, refills steal answers constantly—**empty slots are options**. Don’t fill all three with “maybe later” cards.

## When It’s Worth It

- You can’t afford it this turn but **will buy next turn**
- An opponent will likely take it next turn
- You need gold to close a gap
- In 2-player, denying a high-value engine card

## When It’s Not

- You already hold 2 reserves with vague plans
- Reserving junk just for gold (wastes a slot; reserved cards cannot be discarded)
- You’re too far from the cost—better to keep taking tokens

## Competitive Tips

- Reserving the deck top (unseen to others) can obscure your path
- When an opponent has 3 reserved cards, their buy tempo may slow—your chance to accelerate
