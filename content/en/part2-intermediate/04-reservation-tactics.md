Reserving is not only "locking a card"—it's an information and tempo tool.

## Three Main Uses

1. **Hide intent**: Face-down reserves make the table harder to read (house rules may allow looking at your own reserves)
2. **Snatch key cards**: Unique answers in the display, or cards an opponent needs
3. **Get gold**: When 1 short, reserve for +1 gold and buy next turn

## When It's Worth It

- You can't afford it this turn but **will buy next turn**
- An opponent will likely take it next turn
- You need gold to close a gap
- In 2-player, denying a high-value engine card

## When It's Not

- You already hold 2 reserves with vague plans
- Reserving junk just for gold (wastes a slot)
- You're too far from the cost—better to keep taking gems

## Competitive Tips

- Reserving the deck top (unseen) can **obscure your path**
- When an opponent has 3 reserved cards, their buy tempo may slow—your chance to accelerate
