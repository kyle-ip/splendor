The same strategy has different strength at different player counts.

## 2 Players

- Display turns over slowly; **denial** is more effective
- Noble competition is direct—commit to a path earlier
- Taking 2 same-color for denial happens more often
- Pace can be fast or slow; leans toward **control**

## 3 Players

- Balanced games; engine + nobles mixed is most common
- Watch **both** opponents' bonuses
- Medium reservation competition

## 4 Players

- Display **turns over fast**—good cards vanish quickly
- Noble paths are easily "stolen" by others
- Prefer **decisive buys**; fewer pure gem-taking turns
- Denial still matters, but opportunity cost is higher (while you deny one player, another may score elsewhere)

## Same Opening Board: 2p vs 4p

Use one shared setup; only the player count changes.

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| Nobles | Francis I (emerald×3 ruby×3 onyx×3); Machiavelli (diamond×4 sapphire×4) |
| Level 1 | Cheap emerald, cheap ruby, expensive sapphire mono, stray diamond |
| Bank | Full for the player count |

### Turns 1–3 at 2 players

| Turn | Typical 2p line | Why |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| T1 | Take emerald + ruby + diamond (or 2 emerald if legal and contested) | Slow board; you can afford a soft open |
| T2 | Buy the cheap emerald or ruby | Contested Francis colors; denial ROI is high |
| T3 | Reassess noble vs Machiavelli | Opponent’s tokens are fully readable—pivot early |

### Turns 1–3 at 4 players

| Turn | Typical 4p line | Why |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| T1 | **Buy** the cheapest useful bonus if you can; else take 3 colors toward a buy | Cards vanish before your next turn |
| T2 | Prefer another buy over a pure take | Two other players act between your turns |
| T3 | Lock a path that already shows on the refill | Soft hybrid opens get stolen |

**Takeaway:** 2p rewards **control and denial**; 4p rewards **cashing buys** whenever the display offers them.

## Adjustment Checklist

| Strategy | 2 players | 4 players |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Denial | High | Medium |
| Noble sprint | Viable | Needs display support |
| Hoarding gems | Occasionally OK | Minimize |
| High-point cards | Late sprint OK | Needs engine support |
| Pure gem turns | Common | Expensive—buy when you can |
