Level 1 builds the engine. Levels 2 and 3 are where most games are **decided**—if you buy them too early or too late, you leak tempo.

## What Changes at Each Tier

| Tier | Typical role | Common shapes |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| Level 1 | Bonuses, noble setup | Cheap rainbow costs; many 0-pt |
| Level 2 | Mid points + still-useful bonuses | 1–3 pts; mixed or mono costs |
| Level 3 | Finishers | 3–5 pts; steep costs; buy when the engine pays |

## When a High-Point Card Is the Plan

A 4–5 point card is a **win condition** when:

- Your bonuses already erase most of the cost
- Buying it reaches or crosses 15 **this turn**, or stops an opponent’s safer trigger
- The bonus color still matters for a live noble

It is a **trap** when:

- You still need 2–3 gem turns before you can afford it
- A Level 2 (2–3 pts) + noble finishes the race sooner
- Buying it stalls a contested noble you were one bonus from completing

## Bonus Saturation

After ~3–4 bonuses in a color, another +1 of that color is often weak—unless a **4/4 noble** still needs the fourth.

| Bonus depth | Prefer |
|-------------|--------|
| 0–2 in key colors | More Level 1 / flexible Level 2 |
| 3+ and noble done | Point cards, even off-color |
| Chasing 4/4 | One more matching bonus can be worth more than a random 2-pt |

## Worked Spot

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| Your prestige | 10 |
| Bonuses | emerald ×3, sapphire ×2, ruby ×1 |
| Display | Level 2: 3 pts, +1 diamond, net cost 2; Level 3: 5 pts, +1 emerald, net cost 5 |
| Opponent | 12, acts after you, can buy 3 next turn |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Setup toward the 5 | Opponent may trigger at 15 while you are still collecting |
| Buy the Level 2 now (13) | Stay in the race; next turn look for 2+ or a noble |
| Reserve the 5 | Only if you can cash it before they lock the game |

**Conclusion:** Mid-game, prefer the Level 2 that **scores now** over a Level 3 that needs a multi-turn setup—unless the 5 is a one-turn cash-in.
