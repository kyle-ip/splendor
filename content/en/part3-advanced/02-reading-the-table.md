Splendor is fully open information. Reading the table is a core competitive skill.

## Readable Information

| Information | Inference |
|-------------|-----------|
| Opponent bonuses | Which colors they may buy next |
| Reserve count | Intent to buy next turn |
| Gem count in hand | Purchase power band (10−n ≈ max they can pay) |
| Display gaps | Who urgently needs a given card |

## Reading Steps (15 Seconds Per Turn)

1. Who is **closest to a noble**?
2. Which display card is a **unique answer** critical for an opponent?
3. Who **leads in prestige** and might trigger next turn?
4. Does **my best buy** conflict with someone else's?

## Board Example

Opponent A: 2 sapphire and 2 ruby bonuses, 8 gems in hand, 10 prestige.  
Display: a 4-point card needing 3 sapphire 2 ruby—easy for them.

**Inference**: A will likely buy it next turn. Consider buying it first, reserving it, or taking bank sapphire.

## Caution

Reading the table serves **your decisions**—not denial for its own sake. If snatching a card is useless to you and expensive, stick to your own path.
