**Tempo** is how fast you advance scoring and your engine relative to opponents—are you ahead or behind?

## High Tempo (Accelerate)

- Buy continuously; take gems less often
- Attract nobles early
- Best when: cheap bonus cards are in the display and the noble path is clear

## Low Tempo (Stockpile)

- Take gems toward expensive cards
- Reserve while waiting for better refills
- Best when: 2-player control, or waiting for a key Level 3

## Tempo Switch Signals

**Speed up when**:

- Opponent has ≥12 prestige and you have ≤9
- The display refills a cheap card you need
- A noble is one step away

**Slow down when**:

- Your hand is nearly full with no buy target
- Your only target needs 2 turns of gems
- In 2-player you're ahead by 5+ and can drag their pace

## Worked Spot A — Behind on the clock

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| You | 9 prestige, 6 tokens, can buy a 0-point Level 1 that completes a noble (+3) |
| Opponent | 12 prestige, likely buys a 3 next turn → 15 trigger |
| Alternative | Take gems toward a 5-point card two turns away |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Buy Level 1 → noble | 12 prestige now; you stay in the race and may still act after they trigger |
| Stockpile for the 5 | Opponent likely triggers at 15 while you sit at 9 |

**Conclusion:** When behind on the scoreboard, **cash tempo now** (buy + noble) beats a slower high-point plan.

## Worked Spot B — Lead and freeze

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| You | 14 prestige, flexible hand, lead by 5+ |
| Opponent | 8–9, scrambling for a Level 2 after two gem takes |
| Display | Contested Level 2 they need; you can reserve or buy a soft 2 |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Reserve / deny their answer | Their plan stalls a full turn; your lead holds |
| Force a random Level 3 setup | You overextend; bank gems they needed may refill into their path |

**Conclusion:** With a large lead in 2p, **low tempo + denial** often freezes the race better than maximum-speed buys.

## Counter-example — Don't accelerate into a dead noble

You are at 10 prestige chasing green×4 red×4, but both colors are locked by opponents' bonuses and the last cheap green just got reserved. Forcing more 0-pt engines to finish that noble wastes tempo—**pivot to Level 2 points** instead (same post-noble rule as the sample games).

## Balance Principle

Most games: **mid tempo early, high tempo mid-game, burst or trigger late**. Avoid only taking gems all game or only buying high scorers at random.

Practice tempo switches in [standard practice](/tools/standard) with hints on—watch when the suggested move flips from engine takes to denial or sure points.
