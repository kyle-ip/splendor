A compressed 3-player line highlighting **seat-order pressure**, **shared noble colors**, and **who acts after a trigger**. Seat order: You → West → East.

Compare with the [2-player](/learn/advanced/part3-advanced-07-sample-game-2p) and [4-player](/learn/advanced/part3-advanced-08-sample-game-4p) samples—3p sits between them: bank is thinner than 2p, denial ROI is high, but you still have one “partner” seat that is not racing you every turn.

## Setup Snapshot

| | You | West | East |
|--|-----|------|------|
| Opening intent | Hybrid → 3/3/3 if colors open | Level 2 points + blue | Green/red depth |

**Nobles (4 face-up):** green×3 red×3 black×3; green×4 red×4; white×4 blue×4; red×3 white×3 black×3.

**Bank:** 5 per color + 5 gold (3-player stock).

**Early Level 1:** cheap green, cheap red, cheap black, two blue, one expensive white.

## Snapshot After Turn 5

| | You | West | East |
|--|-----|------|------|
| Bonuses | green 1, red 1 | blue 2 | green 2 |
| Prestige | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Path | Width toward 3/3/3 | Tall blue → Level 2 | Tall green / 4/4 later |

Rehearse: you should be **closer to noble width**; West closer to **early points**; East closer to **contested green**.

## Turns 1–5 — Opening

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (3p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| 1 | You | Take green + red + black | Soft open toward both 3/3/3 tiles |
| 1 | West | Take 2 blue (bank ≥4) | Mono depth; bank already thinner than 2p |
| 1 | East | Take 2 green | Contests your noble color immediately |
| 2 | You | Buy cheap green | Lock a shared color before East stacks it |
| 2 | West | Buy cheap blue | Engine toward Level 2 |
| 2 | East | Buy cheap green | Depth race on green |
| 3 | You | Buy cheap red | Second 3/3/3 color |
| 3 | West | Take toward Level 2 costs | Prefers score pace over noble width |
| 3 | East | Buy second green or reserve your red answer | 3p denial: one reserve can stall your sprint |
| 4 | You | Take gems toward black (third color) | Keep flex—don't pair-take yet |
| 4 | West | Buy Level 2 (~2 pts) if affordable | Points while you chase bonuses |
| 4 | East | Take toward green×4 red×4 | Parallel noble threat |
| 5 | You | Buy cheap black (or reserve if East stole it) | Third 3/3/3 color—or soft pivot |

**Callout:** After T5 you are closer to **green×3 red×3 black×3**; West already has **Level 2 prestige pressure**; East owns **green depth**. Unlike 2p, you cannot ignore the third seat's refill steals.

## Turns 6–10 — Midgame

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (3p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | You | Finish noble gaps / buy third color | Noble sprint is real |
| 6 | West | Cash next Level 2 | Seat after you can punish a slow trigger later |
| 6 | East | Contested reserve on your Level 2 answer | Steal the card that converts your bonuses |
| 7 | You | Attract green×3 red×3 black×3 (+3) after a buy | Efficient scoring; table drops to 3 nobles |
| 7 | West | Deny or score—whichever keeps pace | Don't over-deny free points |
| 7 | East | Buy into green/red for 4/4 | Parallel race |
| 8 | You | Buy flexible Level 2 (2–3 pts)—don't rebuild 0-pt forever | Post-noble pivot (same rule as 2p) |
| 8–10 | West / East | Cash / deny | Three seats compress the race faster than 2p |

**Callout:** Winning the first noble is not winning the game. West's Level 2 cadence often keeps pace with your +3. Count **both** seats after you before you trigger.

## Turns 11–14 — Seat-Order Endgame

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (3p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| 11 | You | Buy to ~12–14 if a 2–3 pt is open | Prefer sure progress over slow Level 3 setup |
| 11–12 | West / East | Same pressure | Anyone can trigger; finish every seat |
| 12 | You | Check: if you hit 15, West and East still act | Seat matrix from [Endgame Calculation](/learn/intermediate/part2-intermediate-06-endgame-calc) |
| 13–14 | All | Cash / deny / tiebreak-aware buys | Card-count ties matter with three buyers |

**Sample finish:** You trigger at 15 → West cashes to 16 → East to 14 → **West wins**. First trigger at 3p often loses if the pack already holds Level 2s.

**Branch:** If East's early reserve denied your T7 noble, pivot to Level 2 economy immediately—don't chase a dead 3/3/3 while two opponents score.

## 3-Player Takeaways

1. **Shared colors are contested early**—expect East/West to race your noble path  
2. **One denial reserve** can stall a noble sprint more cheaply than at 4p (fewer rivals to punish the denier)  
3. **Trigger math is 2 seats deep**—always count West and East after you  
4. Practice the contrast in [standard practice](/tools/standard) at 3 players after reading
