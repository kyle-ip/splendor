This line is a **width open → 3/3/3 noble sprint** while West rushes Level 2 points and East steals refills with reserves. Compare the tempo to the [2-player sample](/learn/advanced/part3-advanced-07-sample-game-2p)—same ideas, four actors, scarcer bank.

## Setup Snapshot

| | You (S) | West | North | East |
|--|---------|------|-------|------|
| Opening intent | 3/3/3 width if colors align | Fast Level 2 points | Emerald/ruby depth | Sapphire flex / denial |

**Nobles (5 face-up):** emerald×3 sapphire×3 onyx×3; emerald×3 ruby×3 onyx×3; emerald×4 ruby×4; diamond×4 sapphire×4; ruby×3 diamond×3 onyx×3.

**Bank:** 7 per color + 5 gold — gems refill slowly; **denial and reserves matter more** than in 2p.

**Early Level 1:** cheap emerald, cheap ruby, cheap onyx, two sapphire, one expensive diamond mono.

| | You | West | North | East |
|--|-----|------|-------|------|

| Prestige | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

Use this table to rehearse: you should be **closer to noble width**; West/North closer to **early Level 2 scores**; East closer to **controlling sapphire and contested reserves**.

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (4p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|

| 1 | West | Take diamond + ruby + sapphire | Points path; not racing your width yet |
| 1 | North | Take 2 emerald (bank ≥4) | Commits emerald depth |
| 1 | East | Take sapphire-heavy mix | Sets up sapphire flex and later denial |
| 2 | You | Buy cheap emerald | Lock first 3/3/3 color before display rotates |
| 2 | West | Buy cheap diamond | Engine toward Level 2 |
| 2 | North | Buy cheap emerald | Depth on the contested color |
| 2 | East | Reserve your cheap ruby answer + 1 gold | 4p denial: cheap insurance against your noble sprint |
| 3 | You | Buy cheap onyx (second color) | Width over mono—bank still deep |
| 3 | West | Take toward Level 2 costs | Prefers score pace over noble width |
| 3 | North | Buy second emerald | Tall emerald threat |
| 3 | East | Take 3 different | Stay flexible; hold gold from reserve |
| 4 | You | Take gems toward ruby (your missing 3/3/3 color) | Don’t pair-take yet—flex beats mono at 4p early |
| 4 | West | Buy cheap ruby | Now races one of your noble colors |
| 4 | North | Reserve a Level 2 emerald-ish | Prepares midgame points |
| 4 | East | Buy cheap sapphire | Depth for denial / Level 2 |
| 5 | You | Buy ruby (or reserve if East stole it) | Third 3/3/3 color—or soft pivot if display dies |
| 5 | West | Cash toward first Level 2 | Points while you chase bonuses |

**Callout:** After T5 you are closer to **emerald×3 ruby×3 onyx×3**; West is closer to **early Level 2 prestige**; East has already spent a turn on **denial reserve**. That reserve is the 4p tax the 2p sample rarely pays this early.

## Turns 6–10 — Contested Midgame

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (4p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | You | Take 3 different (not 2 same) | Bank still deep; finish noble gaps |
| 6 | West | Buy Level 2 (~2 pts) | Score while the table races nobles |
| 6 | North | Buy Level 2 emerald if affordable | Points + depth |
| 6 | East | Contested reserve on next ruby refill | Steal the card that finishes your 3/3/3 |
| 7 | You | Buy onyx/ruby gap card from refill—or pivot | Refills matter every turn at 4p |
| 7 | West | Take toward next Level 2 | Don’t over-deny unless it is free |
| 7 | North | Attract pressure on emerald×4 ruby×4 | Parallel noble race |
| 7 | East | Buy contested ruby from refill (or hold reserve) | Denial ROI is high when three rivals share colors |
| 8 | You | Attract emerald×3 ruby×3 onyx×3 (+3) after a buy | Efficient scoring; table drops to 4 nobles |
| 8 | West | Cash next Level 2 | Noble lead ≠ race win |
| 8 | North | Buy flex Level 2 | Convert depth to points |
| 8 | East | Scan seat order vs your prestige | Plan denial into the final round |
| 9 | You | Buy flexible Level 2 (2–3 pts)—don’t rebuild 0-pt forever | Post-noble pivot (same rule as 2p) |
| 9 | West / North | Cash / deny | Four players compress the race |
| 10 | East | Buy Level 3 if bank is thin and path is clear | Late gems are scarce—don’t wait forever |

**Callout:** Winning the first noble is not winning the game. West’s Level 2 cadence often keeps pace with your +3. The player who **converts bonuses into Level 2 points** after the noble usually leads—same lesson as 2p, with more seats after you on trigger.

## Turns 11–14 — Seat-Order Endgame

| Turn | Actor | Play | Why (4p) |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| 11 | You | Buy to ~12–14 if a 2–3 pt is open | Prefer sure progress over slow Level 3 setup |
| 11–12 | West / North / East | Same pressure | Anyone can trigger; count **all** seats after the leader |
| 12 | You | Check: if you hit 15, West/North/East still act | Seat matrix from [Endgame Calculation](/learn/intermediate/part2-intermediate-06-endgame-calc) |
| 13 | West | Noble or 5-pt if it overtakes | Hidden reserves matter—count them |
| 13–14 | All | Cash / deny / tiebreak-aware buys | Avoid useless 0-pt buys into a card-count tie |

**Sample finish:** You trigger at 15 on your turn → West, North, and East still get final actions. West cashes to 16 → West wins. Seat order at 4p often punishes the first trigger if the pack already has Level 2s in hand.

**Branch:** If East’s early reserve denied your T8 noble, pivot to Level 2 economy immediately—don’t chase a dead 3/3/3 while three opponents score.

## 4-Player Takeaways

1. **Width often beats mono** unless a 4/4 noble is clearly open and uncontested  
2. **Reserves are denial tools**—expect opponents to steal refills you wanted  
3. **Seat on trigger** matters more with four actors in the final round  
4. **Post-noble pivot** to Level 2 is mandatory; width alone rarely races four players
