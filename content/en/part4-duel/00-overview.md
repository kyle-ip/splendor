**Splendor Duel** is a **standalone 2-player game** by Space Cowboys (Marc André & Bruno Cathala). It shares the “buy cards, earn bonuses, score prestige” feel of base Splendor, but the board, actions, and win conditions are different. Do **not** mix Duel components with the base game.

This part teaches Duel on its own. Learn base Splendor first if you can—engine thinking still helps—but treat every rule here as Duel-specific.

## What Stays Familiar

- Buy **Jewel cards** for **bonuses** (permanent discounts) and **prestige**
- **Gold** is a wild token
- **Reserve** cards (max 3) and keep them secret until purchased
- Open information on tokens and purchased cards

## What Changes

| Topic | Base Splendor | Splendor Duel |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Players | 2–4 | Exactly 2 |
| Tokens | Bank piles | Tokens on a **shared board** (spiral) |
| Extra token type | — | **Pearls** (no Pearl bonuses) |
| Side economy | Nobles | **Privileges**, **Crowns**, **Royal cards** |
| Win | Race to 15; finish the round | **One of three** conditions; **game ends immediately** |

## Three Ways to Win

At the **end of your turn**, if you meet **any** of these, you win at once:

1. **≥20 prestige** (total)
2. **≥10 Crowns** (printed on some Jewel cards)
3. **≥10 prestige on cards of one color** (including a “copy color” card stacked on that color)

You do **not** play out the rest of a round like in base Splendor. First to trigger wins.

## Design Intent

Duel is denser and more interactive: taking tokens is geometric, Privileges punish greedy takes, and three win lines force you to watch your rival’s Crowns and monocolor stack—not only their total score.
