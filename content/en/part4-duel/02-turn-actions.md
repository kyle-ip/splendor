Players alternate turns until someone wins. On your turn:

1. Take **none, one, or both** optional actions (**in this order**: Privilege(s), then Replenish)
2. Take **exactly one** mandatory action
3. If you purchased a card: resolve its **ability** (if any); if Crowns reach **3** or **6**, take a **Royal** and resolve it
4. If you have **more than 10** tokens (gems + Pearls + Gold), discard down to 10 (into the bag)
5. Check victory conditions

Keep all tokens visible to your opponent.

## Optional Actions

### 1. Use Privilege(s)

Return **one or more** Privileges to the supply above the board. For **each** returned, take **1 Gem or Pearl** of your choice from the board. **Cannot** take Gold this way.

### 2. Replenish the Board

Only if the **bag is not empty**. Mix bag tokens onto empty spaces from the **center along the spiral** until the bag is empty. Then your **opponent takes 1 Privilege**.

**Stuck case:** If you cannot perform any mandatory action, you **must** perform Replenish before choosing a mandatory action (Replenish itself is illegal if the bag is empty).

## Mandatory Actions (choose one)

### A. Take up to 3 tokens

Take **1–3 adjacent** Gem and/or Pearl tokens in an **uninterrupted** horizontal, vertical, or **diagonal** line. Empty spaces or a **Gold** in the line break the group. You **cannot** take Gold with this action.

- Taking **3 tokens of the same color**, or **2 Pearls**, gives your **opponent 1 Privilege**.

### B. Take 1 Gold and reserve 1 Jewel card

Illegal if there is **no Gold on the board** or you already have **3 reserved** cards.

1. Take **1 Gold** from the board (this is the **only** way to gain Gold).
2. Reserve either **1 face-up pyramid card** or the **top card of any level deck**.

Reserved cards are secret; no effect until purchased. No endgame penalty for leftover reserves. Refill a pyramid slot from its deck when you reserve from the display (if that deck is empty, leave the gap).

### C. Purchase 1 Jewel card

Buy from the **pyramid** or your **reserve**. Pay the printed cost in tokens; **bonuses** reduce matching gem costs (and Pearl costs still need Pearls or Gold—**there are no Pearl bonuses**). Gold is wild for any Gem or Pearl. Spent tokens go to the **bag**. Cost cannot go below 0 (no refunds).

Some cards grant **two bonuses**. Organize purchased cards by bonus color; keep prestige, Crowns, and bonuses visible. Refill pyramid slots from the matching deck when you buy from the display (if that deck is empty, leave the gap). Details on abilities and Royals: [Abilities, Crowns, and Royals](/learn/duel/part4-duel-03-abilities-royals).

## Privilege Supply

Whenever you must take a Privilege and none remain above the board, take one from your opponent (unless you already have all three).
