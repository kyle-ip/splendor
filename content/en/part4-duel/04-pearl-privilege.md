In Duel, **Pearls** and **Privileges** are the tax system around the token board. Mispricing a gift often loses faster than a bad buy.

## Pearls

- Pearls have **no matching bonus**—every Pearl cost must be paid with Pearls or Gold
- Some strong jewels demand Pearls; ignore them until you can cash this turn or next
- Taking **2 Pearls** in one take gifts the opponent **1 Privilege**

## Privilege — How You Get and Spend

| You do… | Opponent gets… |
|---------|----------------|
| Take **3 tokens of the same color** | 1 Privilege |
| Take **2 Pearls** | 1 Privilege |
| **Replenish** the board | 1 Privilege |

Spend Privilege(s) as an **optional** action before your mandatory action: return scroll(s), take that many Gem/Pearl from the board (**not Gold**).

## Worked Spot — Greed tax

| Fact | Value |
|------|-------|
| You | Need 3 blue for a Crown jewel; board has a clean 3-blue line |
| Opp | 0 Privilege, 2 Crowns |

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Take 3 blue | You set up the Crown buy; Opp gains Privilege and may snipe a key Pearl/gem next |
| Take mixed 2 + plan Privilege yourself | Slower Crown, no gift |

**Rule:** Only pay the tax when the buy you enable wins a clock **this turn or next**.

## Worked Spot — Replenish pricing

Board is empty where you need gems. Replenish would gift Privilege.

| Choice | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Replenish then buy | Worth it if the refill **immediately** unlocks a win-clock buy |
| Pass / Privilege-snipe instead | Better when Opp is one Privilege-snipe from spoiling your Crown |

## Counter-example — Pearl greed into Crown loss

You take 2 Pearls to set up a 5-pt. Opp spends the gifted Privilege on the last Crown card, hits 6 Crowns, claims a Royal, and the game ends. Your Pearl setup never acts.

## Checklist Each Take

1. Does this take gift Privilege?
2. Can Opp spend it to spoil my best clock?
3. Is my mandatory buy after this take worth the tax?
