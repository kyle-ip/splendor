Skills from Parts 2–3 (engine, denial, tempo) still matter. Recalibrate for **three win lines**, the **token board**, and **Privileges**.

## Watch All Three Clocks

In base Splendor you mostly track total prestige (and nobles). In Duel, also track:

- Opponent **Crowns** (Royals at the 3rd and 6th Crown; **≥10 Crowns** wins at end of turn)
- Opponent **monocolor prestige** (a quiet 8 in one color is a one-buy threat)
- Your own path: sometimes a Crown rush or a color stack beats racing 20 total

If you only chase 20 points, you will lose to a Crown or monocolor spike.

## Privileges Are Tempo

Privileges let you snipe a needed Gem/Pearl without a full “take tokens” action—and they punish:

- Taking **3 of one color** or **2 Pearls**
- **Replenishing** the board (opponent gains a Privilege)

Ask: is this greedy take worth handing them a Privilege? Often a mixed 2-token line is safer early.

## Board Geometry = Denial

Tokens must form an **uninterrupted** line. Leaving Gold or gaps that break your rival’s best lines is soft denial. Replenish only when you need the refill more than they need the Privilege (or when you are stuck).

## Reserve and Gold

Gold comes **only** from the gold+reserve action, and only while Gold remains on the board. Reserving is still denial (hide a card) and Pearl/wild insurance. Do not hoard three dead reserves if you need board Gold later.

## Engine Still Wins—With a Twist

Bonuses cut costs as in base, but **Pearls never get bonuses**, so Pearl-heavy cards stay token-hungry. Double-bonus cards and copy-color cards accelerate monocolor wins; value them when that line is live.

## Practical Habits

1. After every opponent buy, update Crown / color / total in your head
2. Prefer takes that do **not** gift Privileges unless the tokens finish your buy next turn
3. Decide early whether you are primarily racing **20**, **Crowns**, or **one color**—and spoil the line they are clearly on
4. Treat Royals at 3 and 6 Crowns as tempo spikes (Privilege, points, etc.), not just flavor
